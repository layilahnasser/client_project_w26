Networking at UMSI

Building relationships that shape your career.

Networking at the School of Information is about cultivating meaningful, human connections that help you explore pathways, gain insight, and open doors. Whether you’re just starting to map your career direction or preparing for a job search, UMSI offers a supportive ecosystem to help you connect with alumni, employers, faculty, and peers.

Why networking matters

  • Career clarity: Conversations with professionals help you understand roles, industries, and skills that align with your goals.
  • Hidden opportunities: Many internships and full-time roles are shared through relationships rather than public postings.
  • Real-world insight: Learn what hiring managers look for, how teams work, and what success looks like in your field.
  • Confidence building: Practicing your story and asking thoughtful questions strengthens your professional presence.

Ways to connect

Alumni

UMSI’s alumni network spans UX, data science, policy, research, libraries, healthcare, consulting, and more.

  • UMSI Career Network: Connect with alumni who have opted in to support students.
  • UCAN: Search for alumni open to informational interviews and mentorship.
  • LinkedIn: Explore career paths and reach out with personalized messages.

Employers

UMSI partners with organizations that value information expertise and interdisciplinary skills.

  • Career fairs: Meet recruiters and hiring managers from a wide range of industries.
  • Employer meetups: Join small-group conversations that offer deeper insight into teams and roles.
  • Company site visits: Experience workplace culture and learn how UMSI skills translate on the job.

Peers & faculty

Your classmates and instructors are an important part of your professional network.

  • Student organization events: Build community while exploring shared interests and career paths.
  • Project teams: Collaborate on coursework and experiential learning that can lead to future opportunities.
  • Faculty office hours: Learn from experts who can connect you to research, projects, and industry partners.

How to start a conversation

Networking doesn’t have to feel intimidating. Begin with curiosity and authenticity, and focus on building relationships over time.

  • Introduce yourself clearly: Share your interests, background, and what you’re exploring.
  • Ask thoughtful questions: Focus on their experiences, challenges, and advice.
  • Follow up meaningfully: Send a thank-you note, share updates, and keep the relationship warm.

Tools & resources

  • Email templates: Outreach and follow-up examples for alumni, employers, and peers.
  • Informational interview guides: Question prompts and structures for effective conversations.
  • Networking workshops: Sessions hosted by the Career Development Office to practice and refine your approach.
  • Resume and portfolio reviews: Prepare your materials for conversations with employers and alumni.
