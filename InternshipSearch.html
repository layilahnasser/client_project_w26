From identifying your goals to acing the application process, we've got you covered. Let's dive in and make your internship search a successful journey!

Internships at UMSI

Transform classroom learning into real‑world impact.

Internships are a core part of the UMSI experience, giving students the opportunity to apply information skills in professional environments across UX, data science, libraries, health, policy, and more. The Career Development Office provides tools, coaching, and connections to help you secure meaningful, mission‑aligned internship experiences.

Why internships matter

  • Skill application: Put your coursework into practice through hands‑on projects.
  • Portfolio building: Create artifacts that demonstrate your process, impact, and growth.
  • Career exploration: Test industries, roles, and work environments to refine your direction.
  • Professional connections: Build relationships with mentors, supervisors, and future colleagues.

Finding internships

UMSI resources

  • UMSI Career Development Office — coaching, workshops, and recruiting events.
  • UMSI CareerNet — internship postings curated for UMSI students.
  • UCAN (University Career Alumni Network) — connect with alumni open to mentoring.

University-wide resources

  • U‑M Career Center — campus‑wide internship listings and employer events.
  • LSA Opportunity Hub — internships, funding, and alumni connections.
  • Handshake — thousands of internship postings across industries.

External platforms

  • LinkedIn Jobs — internships and networking in one place.
  • Indeed — broad internship search across sectors.
  • USAJobs — federal internships and pathways programs.
  • Idealist — nonprofit and mission‑driven internship opportunities.

Preparing your application

  • Resume reviews: Meet with a career coach to refine your materials.
  • Portfolio support: Access workshops and feedback sessions to strengthen your case studies.
  • Interview practice: Schedule mock interviews to build confidence and clarity.
  • Offer negotiation guidance: Learn how to evaluate and negotiate internship offers.

Funding opportunities

UMSI and U‑M offer financial support for students pursuing unpaid or low‑paid internships.

  • UMSI Internship Funding — support for experiential learning.
  • CEW+ Scholarships & Grants — funding for nontraditional and underserved students.
  • International Center — CPT/OPT guidance for international students.

Tips for internship success

  • Set clear goals: Identify what skills and experiences you want to gain.
  • Communicate proactively: Check in regularly with your supervisor and ask for feedback.
  • Document your work: Capture artifacts, process notes, and outcomes for your portfolio.
  • Stay connected: Maintain relationships with colleagues after your internship ends.
