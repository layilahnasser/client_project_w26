Networking at UMSI

Building relationships that shape your career.

Introduction

Networking at the School of Information is about cultivating meaningful, human connections that help you explore pathways, gain insight, and open doors. Whether you’re just starting to map your career direction or preparing for a job search, UMSI offers a supportive ecosystem to help you connect with alumni, employers, faculty, and peers.

Why Networking Matters

  • Career clarity: Understand roles, industries, and skills that align with your goals.
  • Hidden opportunities: Many roles are shared through relationships, not job boards.
  • Real-world insight: Learn what hiring managers look for and how teams operate.
  • Confidence building: Practice your story and strengthen your professional presence.

Ways to Connect

Alumni

UMSI’s alumni network spans UX, data science, policy, research, libraries, healthcare, consulting, and more.

  • UMSI Career Network: Connect with alumni who support students.
  • UCAN: Find alumni open to informational interviews and mentorship.
  • LinkedIn: Explore career paths and reach out with personalized messages.

Employers

UMSI partners with organizations that value information expertise and interdisciplinary skills.

  • Career fairs: Meet recruiters and hiring managers.
  • Employer meetups: Join small-group conversations for deeper insight.
  • Company site visits: Experience workplace culture firsthand.

Peers & Faculty

Your classmates and instructors are an important part of your professional network.

  • Student organization events: Build community and explore shared interests.
  • Project teams: Collaborate on coursework and experiential learning.
  • Faculty office hours: Connect with experts who can guide you toward opportunities.

How to Start a Conversation

Networking doesn’t have to feel intimidating. Begin with curiosity and authenticity.

  • Introduce yourself clearly: Share your interests and background.
  • Ask thoughtful questions: Focus on their experiences and advice.
  • Follow up meaningfully: Send a thank-you note and keep the relationship warm.

Tools & Resources

  • Email templates: Outreach and follow-up examples.
  • Informational interview guides: Question prompts and structures.
  • Networking workshops: Sessions hosted by the Career Development Office.
  • Resume and portfolio reviews: Prepare your materials for employer conversations.
